About

25 years from DoD to Fortune 50. Now helping women lead with AI.

I started as a Digital Logic Designer for the Department of Defense. Fresh out of school, building technology for national security applications. That foundation taught me something critical: technology isn't about the tool. It's about the mission.

From there, I spent 18 years at Intel. Rose to Chief of Staff to the CEO. Learned how the world's largest semiconductor company made decisions at the highest level. Sat in rooms where billions of dollars moved on a single technical bet.

Then Microsoft. CTO of Global Partner Solutions. Built and scaled a $B+ AI partner ecosystem. Watched AI transform from an R&D curiosity to a board-level imperative. Led digital transformation initiatives that touched millions of users.

Today, I'm an Independent Director at IDEX Corporation (NYSE: IEX). Co-Founding Advisor at TechArena.ai. And host of ROAR with Lakecia Gunter, a podcast spotlighting leaders who are actually building things.

But here's what I kept seeing: women leaders getting left behind in the AI conversation. Talked over in meetings. Confused by jargon. Pushed to the sidelines of the most important technology shift in our lifetime.

That ends now. I help women executives understand AI, adopt it with confidence, and use it as a competitive advantage. Not a source of anxiety.
